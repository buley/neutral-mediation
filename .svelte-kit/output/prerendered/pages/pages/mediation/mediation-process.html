Doesn’t have as much emphasis in the Pepperdine system

  • Sometimes, more understanding is more repulsion
  • Honor the communication stage, but do not bank on it

Easy to get them to share their story. Hard to get people to understand each other.

Communication and negotiation go together. Eats 70-80% of the time.

Validation

  • acknowledgment of another's existence and importance, assurance that feelings and expressions are being recognized and valued.
  • listener must communicate that he will accept and value what is being communicated

Communication

What are you looking for in the communication phase? Information related to the issues. Lead them toward resolution. Discovering information they need to make a decision. And also what the arbitrator needs to know. Emphasizing the parts that need to be heard.

Pacing is very important. The neutral must hold people in the phase as long as necessary. The longer you can hold them into the Communication phase the more they go from combative to collaborative.  It's best to keep participants out of the negotiation phase for as long as possible: a neutral sometimes slows things down to speed things up.

Mediators and participants communicate via words, intonation, and body language.

Closing

Hard sell vs. consultative selling. During the closing phase, the mediator runs the highest risk of being a bully.

The mediator becomes the salesperson. Someone needs to say the resolution is better than continued fighting. Advocate for resolution.

A small portion of time, but a large part of the value of what people pay you to do.

Outcome:

  • Willingness
  • Safety and hope
  • Expression and understanding
  • Flexibility and innovation
  • Informed Decision

Role of mediator

  • Mediator’s proposal
    • Ask permission from both sides?
    • Point of mutual pain
    • Ask if they accepted if you would accept it
    • If you both say yes, you have a deal. If either says no, neither side finds out that the other said yes.
    • When?
      • Exhausted analysis
      • No way to restart
      • Explored interests without success
      • Waited to see if parties would come up with something
    • “Jump to an agreement” ends back and forth torture. 
    • Could change expectations on what can get even if doesn’t work
    • Sometimes a negotiated mediator’s proposal

Barriers to agreement

  • Lack of information
    • Encourage exchange/discovery
    • Suggest oral representations (vs. whitepapers) with a right of verification on any fact
    • Take advantage of confidentiality
      • “Confidential mediation document”
      • Name
      • Date 
    • Suggest a neutral analysis
  • Disagreement over legal analysis
    • Ask questions
    • Lead analysis
      • Explain hurdles
        • Plaintiff (6% chance):
          • Prove Liability
          • Affirmative Defenses
          • Prove Damages
          • Collection of Damanges
        • Cost of litigation
        • What juries like X or judges like Y are doing
  • Mistrust
    • Promote confidence-building and certainty
    • Reframe risks
  • Format limitations
    • Time limits
    • Parallel discussion tracks
  • Lack of interest-based terms
    • It’s hard to get interested-based terms when the relationship is ending.
    • Complex deals take time
  • Cognitive reasons
    • Loss aversion
      • Losses count 4x gains
      • Framing matters; can reframe
    • Judgmental overconfidence
      • Distance the rider from the horse
  • Emotional reasons
    • Moral/symbolic
